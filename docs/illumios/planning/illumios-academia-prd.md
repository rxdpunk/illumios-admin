# Illumios Academia PRD

**Status:** Active product directive v2  
**Date:** April 14, 2026  
**Owner:** Steve Sposato  
**Business:** Illumios  

## 1. Purpose

This PRD defines the first real product Illumios should build and sell.

The goal is not to create a giant polished academy before customers exist.
The goal is to launch a focused live training offer that helps a specific kind of customer get a real result quickly, so Illumios can learn what resonates, what converts, and what should become the long-term curriculum.

## 2. Product Summary

Illumios will launch a **live training program** for small business owners who want to use AI to save time, reduce busywork, and operate more effectively without hiring more staff.

Canonical active offer name:

**Illumios Academia**

Public-facing subtitle:

**AI for Small Business Owners: Your First 30 Days**

This program is the first paid learning product for Illumios. It will be used to:

- validate demand
- test pricing
- refine the curriculum
- generate testimonials
- identify the best long-term positioning for Illumios Academia

## 2A. Canonical Naming and Cross-Surface Usage

Use this naming across website, admin, and quiz:

- offer name: `Illumios Academia`
- subtitle: `AI for Small Business Owners: Your First 30 Days`

Use discovery calls, quiz outcomes, and admin workflows to support enrollment into this offer.

## 3. Problem Statement

Many small business owners know AI matters, but they do not know where to start, which tools to trust, or how to use AI in a way that creates real business value.

Most of them are experiencing one or more of these problems:

- too much administrative work
- inconsistent follow-up and communication
- no repeatable process for using AI in daily work
- confusion about prompts, tools, and automation
- fear of wasting time on hype instead of getting a result

Existing AI content is often too broad, too technical, or too generic. Small business owners do not need endless tutorials. They need a guided path to one meaningful win.

## 4. Target Customer

### Primary Audience

Small business owners, operators, and solo or lean-team entrepreneurs who:

- are curious about AI
- believe it could help their business
- are not advanced users
- want practical help, not theory
- prefer guided learning over self-teaching

### Secondary Audience

- office managers
- fractional operators
- growth-minded local business owners
- service-based founders with admin-heavy workflows

### Not the Audience for This Program

- enterprise teams
- highly technical builders
- people looking for a done-for-you agency
- people who only want free general AI tips

## 5. Product Goal

The program succeeds if a participant finishes with:

- a clear understanding of where AI fits in their business
- 3 high-value AI use cases relevant to their work
- 1 working workflow, repeatable process, or implemented AI habit
- a 30-day action plan for continued use

## 6. Business Goal

This program exists to create traction and learning, not perfection.

Illumios should use the first cohort to answer the following questions:

1. Will people pay for guided AI training?
2. Which audience segment responds fastest?
3. Which promise converts best?
4. What content creates the strongest student outcomes?
5. What should become the foundation of Illumios Academia?

## 7. Offer Definition

### Offer

A small-group live training experience for business owners who want to begin using AI in practical ways immediately.

### Recommended Format

- 4 live sessions
- 90 minutes per session
- delivered over 2 weeks
- small live cohort
- lightweight Q&A support between sessions

### Suggested Cohort Size

- minimum: 4 people
- target: 6 to 10 people
- maximum for first cohort: 12 people

### Suggested Initial Pricing

Use founding-member pricing.

Recommended test range:

- low test: $297
- target test: $497
- upper test: $697

Default recommendation:

**Launch at $497**

This is high enough to qualify for serious buyers and low enough for a first cohort.

## 8. Core Promise

Canonical offer promise:

**In 4 live sessions, small business owners will identify their best AI opportunities, build one working workflow, and leave with a practical 30-day plan without needing to become technical experts.**

What participants leave with:

- clarity on where AI can actually help in their business
- 3 prioritized AI use cases relevant to their work
- 1 simple tool and prompt stack they can use immediately
- 1 meaningful implementation win in the form of a working workflow
- confidence to keep going with a practical 30-day plan

Short public version:

**Learn how to use AI in your business in 30 days by building one practical workflow you can actually keep using.**

- one clear transformation promise

## 10. Curriculum Outline

## Session 1: Find Your First AI Wins

### Goal

Help participants understand where AI creates real value and identify their best first opportunities.

### Topics

- what AI is good at
- what AI is not good at
- where small businesses waste time
- identifying repetitive, high-friction tasks
- choosing one meaningful starting point

### Output

Each participant leaves with a list of 3 business use cases worth testing.

## Session 2: Use the Right Tools and Prompts

### Goal

Help participants stop guessing and start using AI tools deliberately.

### Topics

- how to choose tools simply
- prompt basics for business tasks
- prompt structure that gets better outputs
- common mistakes and how to correct them
- examples for operations, marketing, and communication

### Output

Each participant leaves with a simple tool stack and 3 reusable prompts tailored to their work.

## Session 3: Build Your First Workflow

### Goal

Help participants create one repeatable AI-assisted workflow.

### Topics

- turning a repeated task into a simple process
- where AI fits inside a workflow
- lightweight automation vs manual AI usage
- documenting the process so it can be reused
- implementation examples

### Output

Each participant leaves with one working AI-supported workflow or process.

## Session 4: Make It Stick for 30 Days

### Goal

Help participants turn momentum into adoption.

### Topics

- what to keep doing weekly
- how to measure whether AI is helping
- how to build team habits
- when to add new tools
- what to avoid next

### Output

Each participant leaves with a personal 30-day action plan.

### Delivery Structure for All Sessions

Each live session should follow the same basic rhythm:

- short teaching block with examples
- guided working time
- live feedback and Q&A
- one concrete participant deliverable completed before the next session

## 11. User Journey

1. Prospect hears about Illumios through a talk, referral, networking event, outreach, or the website.
2. Prospect sees a simple invitation to `Illumios Academia` and understands the promise quickly.
3. Prospect completes the website application.
4. Prospect completes the fit quiz so Illumios can assess role, pain points, and readiness.
5. Qualified prospect books an enrollment call.
6. Illumios uses the call to confirm fit, explain outcomes, answer questions, and invite the right participant to join the program.
7. Participant enrolls and pays.
8. Participant receives onboarding message, schedule, expectations, and simple pre-work.
9. Participant attends live sessions.
10. Participant completes one meaningful implementation win.
11. Participant gives feedback and testimonial.
12. Participant is offered next-step support, strategy session, or future Academia enrollment.

## 14. Risks and Mitigations

### Risk: The offer feels too broad

Mitigation:

- keep the promise tied to one clear audience and one clear result
- avoid teaching "all of AI"

### Risk: The course becomes too big before launch

Mitigation:

- teach live first
- do not build a full academy before running the first cohort

### Risk: People are interested but do not buy

Mitigation:

- test the promise with real conversations
- use founding-member pricing
- sell the transformation, not the syllabus

### Risk: Students get inspired but do not implement

Mitigation:

- require one specific output per session
- keep assignments practical and small
