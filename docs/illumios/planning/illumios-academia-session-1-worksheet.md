## What You Will Leave With

By the end of this session, you should have:

- 3 AI use cases worth testing in your business

## Ground Rule

Do not try to identify every possible AI use case in your business.

Your goal is to find the best first ones:

- repeated
- annoying
- useful
- low-risk
- easy to review

## Part 7: Low-Risk Check

Answer these before locking your first use case.

Can I review the output before I use it?

Would a rough first draft still be helpful here?

If AI made a mistake, would the downside be manageable?

If the answer is "no" to most of these, choose a lower-risk first use case.

## Part 8: Bring This to Session 2

Bring one real example related to your best first use case.

Good examples:

- an email you need to write
- a customer question you answer often
- meeting notes
- a proposal draft
- intake notes
- rough content or bullet points

Do not bring a polished version.
Bring the messy real thing.
