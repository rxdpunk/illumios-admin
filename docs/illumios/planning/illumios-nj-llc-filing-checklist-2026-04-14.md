# Illumios NJ LLC Filing Checklist

Date: April 14, 2026
Purpose: Practical checklist for forming `Illumios LLC` in New Jersey without deleting or replacing the longer strategy memo

Status: Reference checklist. Use alongside the New Jersey LLC decision and the current operating directives.

## Outcome

The goal is to form `Illumios LLC` as a New Jersey LLC, get the company legally operational, and make sure the governance documents match the founders' real roles.

## Phase 1: Pre-Filing Decisions

### 1. Confirm the company name

- Confirm that `Illumios LLC` is available in New Jersey.
- Confirm whether the business will also need a domain or DBA strategy.

### 2. Decide basic entity structure

- Confirm that the entity will be an LLC.
- Confirm the initial founders or members.
- Decide whether the company should be `member-managed` or `manager-managed`.

Working assumption based on current facts:

- A `manager-managed` structure may be cleaner if Steve is the primary operating founder and Sunshine wants a more behind-the-scenes posture for now.

This should still be confirmed with startup counsel.

### 3. Decide the company address and registered agent

- Decide the principal business address to use.
- Decide whether to use Steve's address, another business address, or a commercial mailbox where appropriate.
- Decide whether to serve as your own New Jersey registered agent or hire a registered agent service.

### 4. Clarify founder roles before filing

- Confirm Steve's working role as the primary operator.
- Confirm Sunshine's current role and preferred visibility.
- Decide whether Sunshine should immediately hold an officer-style title publicly, or whether that should wait pending employment review.

## Phase 2: New Jersey Formation Steps

### 5. File the New Jersey LLC formation

- File the New Jersey certificate of formation for `Illumios LLC`.
- Pay the current New Jersey filing fee.
- Save the stamped filing confirmation and certificate.

### 6. Get the EIN

- Apply for the company's EIN with the IRS.
- Save the EIN confirmation notice.

### 7. Complete NJ-REG

- Complete New Jersey tax and employer registration.
- Confirm whether there will be payroll now or later.
- Confirm whether sales tax registration is required for current or planned offerings.

### 8. Create the compliance folder

Set up a secure internal folder with:

- formation certificate
- EIN confirmation
- NJ-REG confirmation
- operating agreement
- cap table or equity tracker
- founder IP assignment agreements
- contractor templates
- banking records

## Phase 3: Operating Agreement Checklist

This is the most important internal document after formation.

The operating agreement should match reality, not just aspiration.

### 9. Ownership and capital

- Confirm each founder's ownership percentage.
- Confirm whether any founder is contributing cash, IP, equipment, or unpaid labor as initial capital.
- Document whether future contributions are required or optional.

### 10. Vesting

- Decide whether founder equity vests over time.
- Decide start date, cliff, and acceleration terms if any.
- Decide what happens if a founder leaves early.

### 11. Management structure

- Decide member-managed vs. manager-managed.
- Define who can sign contracts.
- Define who can open bank accounts.
- Define who can hire contractors or employees.
- Define which decisions require unanimous founder approval.

### 12. Role clarity

Document the intended operating reality clearly:

- Steve handles day-to-day leadership, public-facing communication, and primary execution.
- Sunshine supports strategy and internal operational continuity in a more behind-the-scenes role for now.

### 13. IP ownership

- Make sure all founder-created work product is assigned to the LLC.
- Include invention assignment and confidentiality obligations.
- Make sure no company IP is left in personal ownership by mistake.

### 14. Departures and disputes

- Define what happens if a founder leaves.
- Define repurchase rights if unvested equity exists.
- Define deadlock resolution rules.
- Define transfer restrictions.

## Phase 4: Employment and Risk Review

### 15. Review Sunshine's outside-employment constraints

Before expanding Sunshine's formal role, review:

- employment agreement
- confidentiality agreement
- invention assignment agreement
- handbook moonlighting rules
- any conflict-of-interest policy

### 16. Operating discipline while Sunshine remains employed elsewhere

- No Illumios work on TurnPoint devices or systems.
- No TurnPoint confidential information used in Illumios work.
- No Illumios work on TurnPoint time.
- Be careful with public title usage until counsel reviews the situation.

## Phase 5: Post-Formation Operations

### 17. Banking

- Open the business bank account after formation and EIN issuance.
- Do not mix company funds with personal funds.

### 18. Accounting and tax setup

- Choose bookkeeping software or accountant.
- Decide who will maintain books.
- Confirm tax classification assumptions with a CPA.
- Decide whether the default LLC tax treatment is fine for now.

### 19. Contracts

- Put founder IP assignments in place.
- Put contractor agreements in place before using outside builders.
- Use company-name contracting once the LLC is active.

### 20. Annual compliance tracking

- Calendar New Jersey annual report deadlines.
- Calendar tax deadlines.
- Reassess South Carolina registration if Sunshine's role becomes more active there.

## South Carolina Reassessment Triggers

Revisit South Carolina foreign qualification quickly if any of these become true:

- Sunshine begins active operational management from South Carolina.
- Illumios hires workers in South Carolina.
- Illumios opens an office or regular place of business there.
- customer delivery, sales activity, or execution becomes materially centered there.

## Recommended Immediate Action List

1. Confirm the name and New Jersey availability.
2. Decide on registered agent approach.
3. File the NJ LLC.
4. Obtain EIN.
5. Complete NJ-REG.
6. Draft and sign the operating agreement.
7. Review Sunshine's employment-related constraints.
8. Open bank account and start clean bookkeeping.
