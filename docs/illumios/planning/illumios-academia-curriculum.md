# Illumios Academia Curriculum

Last updated: 2026-04-14  
Status: first teachable curriculum draft for live delivery

## What This Program Actually Teaches

This program does not teach "all of AI."

It teaches small business owners how to do four things:

1. identify where AI can actually help in their own business
2. get better outputs from a small, simple tool stack
3. turn one repeated task into a repeatable AI-assisted workflow
4. keep using that workflow for the next 30 days

That is the curriculum spine.

## What You Are Really Selling

You are not selling AI knowledge.

You are selling:

- relief from busywork
- better judgment about where AI fits
- one practical implementation win
- confidence to keep going without overwhelm

## What Not To Teach in the First Cohort

Avoid spending time on:

- AI history, model comparisons, or industry news
- coding, APIs, or advanced automation setup
- giant tool tours
- abstract theory about prompts without business examples
- trying to solve every department in one cohort

If a topic does not help participants leave with one working workflow and a 30-day plan, it is not core curriculum.

## Core Teaching Framework

Every session should reinforce the same operating loop:

1. pick one real business task
2. define the desired output
3. use AI to draft, organize, or transform
4. review with human judgment
5. save the process so it can be repeated
6. measure whether it actually saves time or improves consistency

This gives participants one simple mental model they can reuse after the program ends.

## Suggested Default Tool Stack

Keep the tool stack intentionally simple:

- one general AI chat tool the participant can access now
- Google Docs or Word for storing prompts and workflows
- optional spreadsheet for tracking tasks and time saved

The teaching goal is not tool sophistication. The goal is repeatable business use.

## Good Use-Case Categories To Teach

Prioritize examples that are common across small businesses:

- email drafting and follow-up
- meeting notes and next-step summaries
- proposal or estimate drafts
- customer FAQ and response templates
- social or newsletter repurposing
- internal SOP drafting
- lead follow-up messaging
- intake summaries or client recap notes

## Session 1: Find Your First AI Wins

### Outcome

Each participant leaves with 3 prioritized AI use cases worth testing in their real business.

### Teaching Goal

Move people from vague curiosity about AI to a short, ranked list of realistic opportunities.

### 90-Minute Agenda

1. Welcome and program framing - 10 minutes
2. What AI is good at for small businesses - 10 minutes
3. Where business owners lose time every week - 10 minutes
4. Live exercise: task inventory and friction audit - 20 minutes
5. Teach a prioritization method - 10 minutes
6. Live exercise: choose top 3 use cases - 20 minutes
7. Share-outs and feedback - 10 minutes

### Core Teaching Points

- AI is best at drafting, organizing, summarizing, rewriting, and patterning repeated work
- AI is weak when the task needs final judgment, sensitive decisions, or deep business context without review
- the best first wins are frequent, annoying, low-risk tasks
- participants should start where time is already leaking, not where AI seems flashy

### Framework To Teach

Teach a simple scoring method for each possible use case:

- Frequency: how often does this happen?
- Friction: how annoying or slow is it?
- Risk: how safe is it to test with AI?
- Value: if this improved, would it matter?

Tell them to start with tasks that are:

- frequent
- annoying
- low-risk
- easy to review

### Live Exercise

Prompt participants to list:

- 5 tasks they repeat every week
- 3 tasks they dread
- 2 tasks they often procrastinate because they take too long

Then have them rank their top 3 candidates.

### Example Demos

- turning a messy email into a polished client follow-up
- summarizing a meeting transcript into action items
- converting rough notes into a reusable FAQ answer

### Homework

- bring one real example of their top use case to Session 2
- examples can be a draft email, notes, a proposal, intake text, or customer question

## Session 2: Use the Right Tools and Prompts

### Outcome

Each participant leaves with one simple tool stack and 3 reusable prompts tailored to their work.

### Teaching Goal

Help participants stop treating AI like a magic box and start giving it useful instructions.

### 90-Minute Agenda

1. Review Session 1 wins and homework examples - 10 minutes
2. Teach the simple tool stack - 10 minutes
3. Teach prompt anatomy - 15 minutes
4. Live prompt makeover demo - 15 minutes
5. Guided work: write Prompt 1 for their top task - 15 minutes
6. Guided work: improve Prompt 1 after seeing output - 10 minutes
7. Guided work: create Prompt 2 and Prompt 3 - 10 minutes
8. Share-outs and troubleshooting - 5 minutes

### Core Teaching Points

- bad prompts are usually vague, not "wrong"
- participants should tell AI the role, context, task, constraints, and desired output
- the first output is a draft, not the final answer
- better prompts come from iteration and clearer business context

### Prompt Structure To Teach

Teach this exact template:

- Role: who should the AI act like?
- Context: what business situation is this for?
- Task: what exactly do you want created?
- Constraints: what tone, length, or rules matter?
- Output format: what should the final answer look like?

### Three Prompt Types To Build

Every participant should leave with:

1. one prompt for drafting
2. one prompt for rewriting or improving
3. one prompt for summarizing or organizing

### Example Demo

Show a weak prompt such as:

`write a follow-up email`

Then improve it step by step into:

- role
- business context
- who the customer is
- desired tone
- specific CTA
- short length

### Homework

- use at least one of the 3 prompts in real work before Session 3
- bring the original task, AI output, and final version they actually used

## Session 3: Build Your First AI Workflow

### Outcome

Each participant leaves with one working AI-assisted workflow or repeatable process.

### Teaching Goal

Turn isolated prompt usage into a repeatable business process.

### 90-Minute Agenda

1. Review examples from Session 2 homework - 10 minutes
2. Teach what makes a workflow repeatable - 10 minutes
3. Break down one task into steps - 15 minutes
4. Demo: workflow design for a common SMB task - 15 minutes
5. Guided build: participants map their own workflow - 20 minutes
6. Guided build: define review points and final output - 10 minutes
7. Pair or group feedback - 10 minutes

### Core Teaching Points

- a workflow is not "use AI sometimes"
- a workflow has a trigger, steps, review point, and final output
- human review stays wherever accuracy, judgment, or client sensitivity matters
- the best first workflow is simple enough to repeat next week

### Workflow Template To Teach

Use this exact structure:

- Trigger: when does this task start?
- Input: what information comes in?
- AI step: what does AI generate or transform?
- Human review: what does the owner check or edit?
- Final output: what gets sent, saved, or used?
- Storage: where does the prompt or SOP live for next time?

### Demo Workflow Ideas

Pick one or two of these:

- inquiry received -> AI drafts response -> owner edits -> send
- meeting held -> notes pasted into AI -> summary and next steps -> saved and sent
- service request arrives -> AI drafts estimate/proposal -> owner reviews -> send

### Homework

- run the workflow once in real life before Session 4
- track rough time spent before and after
- note what broke, what felt awkward, and what actually helped

## Session 4: Make It Stick for 30 Days

### Outcome

Each participant leaves with a 30-day implementation plan and a clear next-step rhythm.

### Teaching Goal

Prevent the classic outcome where people get excited in class and then never use what they built again.

### 90-Minute Agenda

1. Review workflow results from Session 3 - 15 minutes
2. Teach how to measure whether AI is helping - 10 minutes
3. Teach the 30-day adoption plan - 15 minutes
4. Guided work: fill out a personal 30-day implementation plan - 25 minutes
5. Share commitments and likely obstacles - 15 minutes
6. Wrap-up, feedback, and next-step invitation - 10 minutes

### Core Teaching Points

- success is not using AI everywhere
- success is using one workflow consistently enough to create a real win
- time saved, consistency improved, or faster response time are enough for first-cohort success
- add a second workflow only after the first one becomes normal

### 30-Day Plan Template To Teach

Each participant should define:

- one workflow they will keep using
- how often they will use it each week
- what metric they will watch
- where the prompt and SOP will be stored
- what the next likely workflow will be after this first one sticks

### Good Simple Metrics

- minutes saved each time
- response time reduced
- number of times the workflow was used
- consistency of communication
- reduction in blank-page drafting time

## What Steve Should Actually Say in Class

Use language like:

- "We are not trying to turn you into a technical expert."
- "We are looking for one useful win, not a total reinvention of your business."
- "The best first AI use case is usually boring, repeated, and annoying."
- "If you still need to review it, that is normal. AI is helping you move faster, not replacing your judgment."
- "The goal is not better prompts for their own sake. The goal is better business output."

## Default Examples You Can Reuse Across Cohorts

Keep a small bank of examples ready:

- client follow-up email
- estimate or proposal draft
- meeting summary with action items
- FAQ answer bank
- social post from existing content
- intake notes to organized summary

These are flexible enough to feel relevant to many small business owners.

## Minimum Materials To Prepare

Before teaching, create:

- one worksheet for Session 1 task inventory and prioritization
- one prompt template handout for Session 2
- one workflow builder worksheet for Session 3
- one 30-day implementation plan worksheet for Session 4
- a small library of example prompts and before/after outputs

## The Real Outcome Standard

If a participant finishes the program and says:

"I know exactly where AI helps me, I have one workflow I can use this week, and I am not intimidated anymore,"

then the curriculum worked.
