## Executive Summary

Based on the current operating facts, the recommended path is to form `Illumios LLC` as a **New Jersey domestic LLC**, not a Wyoming LLC.

The reason is practical rather than theoretical:

- The business is currently being operated primarily by Steve from New Jersey.
- Steve is full-time on Illumios and is effectively running day-to-day operations.
- Sunshine is currently employed full-time by TurnPoint Solutions and is not the primary public-facing operator for Illumios.
- On the current facts, Wyoming would likely add an extra layer of administration rather than reduce it, because New Jersey would still likely need to be treated as an operating state.

At this stage, New Jersey appears to be the cleanest and most defensible home state for formation.

## Bottom-Line Recommendation

### Recommended now

Form `Illumios LLC` in **New Jersey**.

### Recommended structure to discuss with counsel

Use a **manager-managed** or otherwise carefully drafted operating agreement that matches the founders' intended roles:

- Steve as the primary operating founder, public-facing leader, and day-to-day decision-maker
- Sunshine as a strategic, behind-the-scenes co-founder with a more limited public profile for now

### Not recommended as the default

Do **not** form in Wyoming unless there is a specific Wyoming-driven objective that outweighs the extra maintenance burden.

Examples of the kind of reasons that might justify Wyoming:

- a deliberate privacy strategy advised by counsel
- a specific investor, asset-protection, or holding-company structure
- a plan to keep actual operations out of New Jersey and South Carolina

None of those reasons appear to be the current fact pattern.

## Decision Framework

When choosing an LLC formation state for a small founder-led startup, the key question is usually not "Which state is cheapest to form?" It is:

**Where is the business actually being operated?**

That matters because if the company forms in one state but is actually operating in another, the company often must:

- foreign-register in the operating state
- maintain registered agents in multiple states
- manage multiple annual filing calendars
- handle extra tax and compliance complexity

This is why a Wyoming recommendation often sounds attractive in founder communities but becomes inefficient in practice for a founder actually running the business from New Jersey.

## Why New Jersey Is the Best Fit on These Facts

### 1. New Jersey is the current center of gravity

The most important fact is that Steve is full-time on Illumios and operating from New Jersey. On the present record, New Jersey is the actual management and execution base of the company.

That makes New Jersey the most natural state for:

- principal business activity
- tax and employer registration
- banking and contractual operations
- registered agent alignment
- future hiring and operational scaling

### 2. Forming in Wyoming would probably not eliminate New Jersey compliance

New Jersey requires businesses doing business in New Jersey to register. New Jersey also makes clear that legal entities such as LLCs generally need both:

- a formation or authorization filing, and
- the NJ-REG tax/employer registration

If Illumios were formed in Wyoming but operated from New Jersey, New Jersey would likely still remain a live compliance state. In practice, that means Wyoming would likely become an **extra** state, not a substitute state.

### 3. New Jersey formation and New Jersey foreign-registration cost the same base state filing fee

New Jersey currently charges:

- `$125` for a certificate of formation
- `$125` for a foreign LLC registration

So if the company forms in Wyoming first and then still needs authority in New Jersey, the business does not avoid the main New Jersey state filing fee. It simply adds a Wyoming layer on top.

### 4. Annual maintenance is simpler if the home state matches the real operating state

New Jersey annual reports are currently `$75` each year.

Wyoming annual reports for LLCs carry:

- a minimum annual fee of `$60`, and
- a Wyoming registered agent requirement

If a Wyoming LLC still has to register or operate in New Jersey, the company could end up maintaining both:

- Wyoming annual compliance, and
- New Jersey compliance

That is usually not worth it at this stage.

## Why Wyoming Is Not the Best Default Choice Here

Wyoming is often marketed as:

- low cost
- founder friendly
- privacy oriented
- simple

Those benefits are most useful when the business does not have a stronger real operating nexus elsewhere.

For Illumios, the issue is not whether Wyoming is valid. It is. The issue is whether Wyoming improves the founders' real compliance position. On current facts, it likely does not.

### The main Wyoming downside for Illumios

If Steve is running the business from New Jersey full-time, Wyoming likely creates a "double-layer" outcome:

1. form in Wyoming
2. then still register or comply in New Jersey

That generally means:

- more paperwork
- more calendar management
- more registered-agent overhead
- more chances to miss a filing

### Why not South Carolina

Sunshine lives in South Carolina, but she is not currently the full-time operating founder. She is not described as the primary public-facing operator, principal manager, or person currently running day-to-day company execution.

If Sunshine's role grows into a more regular operating role from South Carolina, or if the company later has:

- meaningful business operations centered there
- regular contract execution there
- a South Carolina office or operational base

then South Carolina foreign qualification, payroll, and tax obligations may become more important.

### Current likely posture

Based on the facts provided, South Carolina looks more like a **watch list state** than the best formation state.

This is an inference from the facts and not a formal legal conclusion. South Carolina counsel or startup counsel should confirm the threshold for foreign qualification if Sunshine's role becomes more active.

## Foreign Qualification Risk by State

### New Jersey

New Jersey is the strongest candidate for immediate domestic formation because it is the state most closely tied to actual business operations.

If the company were formed elsewhere, New Jersey would be the first state most likely to still matter.

### South Carolina

South Carolina may or may not require foreign qualification immediately depending on how active Sunshine's role is in practice.

Key triggers to reassess South Carolina quickly:

- Sunshine becomes an employee or officer with active operational duties from South Carolina
- customer work, revenue generation, or delivery becomes centered in South Carolina
- the company hires in South Carolina
- a South Carolina mailing address, office, or regular place of business is used

### Wyoming

Wyoming only makes sense if it is deliberately chosen for a separate structural reason and the founders are comfortable managing a multi-state structure from the start.

## Sensitive Founder-Role Risk: Sunshine's Current Employment

This is one of the most important issues in the entire memo.

Sunshine is currently full-time at TurnPoint Solutions and would prefer to keep her Illumios role relatively discreet for now.

That creates a separate risk category unrelated to Wyoming vs. New Jersey:

- employment conflict rules
- moonlighting restrictions
- confidentiality obligations
- invention assignment provisions
- non-solicitation or non-compete issues where applicable
- misuse of employer time, systems, or confidential information

### Practical implication

The founders should treat this as a **formation-and-governance issue**, not just a personal preference issue.

### Recommended founder discipline

- Do not use TurnPoint devices, accounts, software, systems, or paid work hours for Illumios work.
- Do not allow any Illumios materials to incorporate TurnPoint confidential information.
- Review Sunshine's employment agreement, handbook, invention assignment agreement, and any side-letter restrictions.
- Have startup counsel confirm what titles, equity arrangements, and involvement levels are safest while she remains employed elsewhere.

### Structuring implication

The operating agreement should reflect the real-world division of labor instead of pretending both founders are equally public and equally operational today.

That does not diminish Sunshine's value. It simply reduces legal and factual mismatch.

## Governance Recommendation

- founder disputes are less likely when responsibilities are documented early

## Tax and Filing Notes

### New Jersey

For an LLC, New Jersey currently requires:

- business formation or authorization
- NJ-REG registration for tax/employer purposes
- annual reports

### South Carolina

South Carolina requires registration before conducting business there, and out-of-state businesses receive a certificate of authority.

South Carolina also flags remote-worker compliance for out-of-state businesses with South Carolina workers.

For South Carolina annual-report/license-fee purposes, a key official tax ruling states that an LLC that is **not taxed as a corporation** is not subject to the annual report or license fee requirements described for corporations.

### Wyoming

Wyoming currently requires:

- `$100` filing fee for domestic LLC formation
- a Wyoming registered agent
- annual reports due each year
- annual fee of `$60` minimum or more depending on Wyoming assets
